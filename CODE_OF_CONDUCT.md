# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and inclusive community focused on advancing scientific understanding of cold fusion and LENR (Low Energy Nuclear Reactions). We welcome contributors regardless of background, experience level, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, personal appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality.

## Our Standards

### ✅ Expected Behavior

**Scientific Integrity**
- Present evidence objectively and cite sources accurately
- Distinguish between established facts, hypotheses, and personal opinions
- Respect different interpretations of scientific data
- Acknowledge uncertainties and limitations in research

**Community Interaction**
- Use welcoming, inclusive, and respectful language
- Show empathy and kindness to other community members
- Give constructive feedback and gracefully accept criticism
- Focus on what's best for the project and scientific progress

**Collaboration**
- Be patient with questions and provide helpful responses
- Respect different experience levels and perspectives
- Credit others' work and contributions appropriately
- Help newcomers feel welcome and supported

### ❌ Unacceptable Behavior

**Harmful Conduct**
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory language
- Trolling, inflammatory comments, or deliberate disruption
- Doxxing or sharing private information without consent

**Scientific Misconduct**
- Deliberate misrepresentation of scientific data or sources
- Plagiarism or failing to credit original work
- Spreading misinformation as established fact
- Suppressing legitimate scientific discussion through intimidation

**Disruptive Behavior**
- Spam, off-topic discussions, or excessive self-promotion
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances  
- Publishing others' private communications without permission
- Any other conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking fair and appropriate corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting content that violates this code
- Temporarily or permanently banning contributors for inappropriate behavior

Decisions will be made based on:
- **Severity** of the violation
- **Intent** behind the behavior  
- **Impact** on the community
- **Pattern** of behavior over time

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project repositories and communication channels
- Issue discussions, pull requests, and code reviews
- Community events, conferences, and social media interactions
- Any space where someone represents the project

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Document the incident** with screenshots or links if possible
2. **Report via GitHub issues** (public incidents) or **email the maintainers** (private matters)
3. **Provide context** - what happened, when, and who was involved
4. **Be patient** - we will review all reports carefully and respond appropriately

Reports will be:
- **Reviewed promptly** by project maintainers
- **Kept confidential** when requested
- **Handled fairly** without retaliation against reporters

## Enforcement

Violations may result in:

**First violation**: Warning and educational resources
**Repeat violations**: Temporary ban from participation  
**Severe violations**: Permanent ban from the project

We reserve the right to remove any contribution that violates this code, regardless of technical merit.

## Scientific Debate vs Personal Attack

**Encouraged**: "The data in this study appears incomplete because..."
**Not acceptable**: "This author is clearly biased and incompetent..."

**Encouraged**: "Have you considered alternative explanations like...?"
**Not acceptable**: "Anyone who believes this is obviously ignorant..."

We welcome robust scientific debate while maintaining respect for people.
